The Shiipp Warehouse platform is built around four interconnected entities that mirror the real-world lifecycle of air-cargo freight forwarding. Every action you take — from scanning a package at intake to generating a customer invoice — flows through this shared data model. Understanding how these entities relate to one another gives you a clear mental map of the platform before you dive into any individual feature.

## Core Entity Relationships

The platform models freight forwarding as a pipeline. Each step produces data that the next step consumes, creating a traceable, auditable chain from the moment a package enters your warehouse to the moment your customer is billed.

| Step | Entity | What Happens |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Courier Partner** | A logistics carrier is onboarded with branding, AWB sequences, and API credentials |
| 2 | **Package Intake** | Individual parcels are logged, weighed, and assigned to a courier partner |
| 3 | **Flight Manifest** | Packages are grouped under a Master AWB for a specific outbound flight |
| 4 | **Invoice / Billing** | Consolidated charges are calculated from manifest weights and courier rates |

Reading top to bottom, the flow looks like this:

1. You onboard a **Courier Partner** (e.g., AmeriJet, Caribbean Airlines) with its AWB prefix and branding.
2. Packages arrive at your warehouse and are logged as **Package** records, each assigned to a courier.
3. When a flight is ready, you create a **Flight Manifest** — a container that groups all packages traveling under the same Master AWB.
4. Once the manifest is finalized, the billing engine generates an **Invoice** for the relevant shipper or end customer.

## Multi-Tenant Architecture

Shiipp Warehouse is a fully multi-tenant platform. Each organization operates in a completely isolated environment:

- **Data isolation** — Package records, manifests, and invoices are scoped to your organization and are never visible to other tenants.
- **User management** — You control which team members have access and at what permission level.
- **Courier partners** — Each organization maintains its own set of courier integrations, AWB sequences, and API credentials. A courier onboarded in one workspace has no relationship to the same carrier in another workspace.
- **Branding** — Tracking pages and printed documents reflect your organization's courier branding, not a shared Shiipp identity.

This architecture means you can safely onboard multiple courier partners, manage high package volumes, and share API credentials with partners — all without any cross-tenant data leakage.
